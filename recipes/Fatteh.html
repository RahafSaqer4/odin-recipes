Fattet Hummus

Description

Fattet hummus is a delicious and hearty Middle Eastern dish that layers crispy pita bread, creamy hummus, tangy yogurt, and toasted nuts. It's a popular breakfast or brunch choice, often enjoyed during Ramadan. The combination of textures and flavors makes it a satisfying and comforting meal.

Ingredients

  • For the Hummus:
    • 1 can (15 ounces) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
    • 1/4 cup tahini
    • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1/4 cup water
  • For the Fatteh:
    • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
    • 1/4 cup toasted pine nuts
    • 1/4 cup olive oil
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground sumac
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
    • 1/2 cup crumbled pita bread, toasted
    • Fresh parsley, for garnish
    • A few tablespoons of the liquid from the canned chickpeas

Steps

  1. Prepare the Hummus:
    1. Combine Ingredients: In a food processor, combine chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, cumin, salt, and water.
    2. Blend: Process until smooth and creamy, adding more water as needed to reach your desired consistency.
  2. Prepare the Fatteh:
    1. Toast Pita Bread: Cut pita bread into small pieces and toast in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for 5-7 minutes, or until golden brown.
    2. Mix Yogurt Topping: In a bowl, combine Greek yogurt, pine nuts, olive oil, sumac, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Assemble the Fatteh:
    1. Layer 1 (Base): Spread a layer of toasted pita bread in a deep dish.
    2. Layer 2 (Soaking): Pour a few tablespoons of the liquid from the canned chickpeas over the bread to soften it.
    3. Layer 3 (Hummus): Spread a layer of the prepared hummus over the softened bread.
    4. Layer 4 (Yogurt Topping): Top with the yogurt mixture.
    5. Garnish: Sprinkle with toasted pine nuts, sumac, and fresh parsley.
    6. Serve Immediately

Enjoy the warm, flavorful layers of this traditional Syrian dish.
